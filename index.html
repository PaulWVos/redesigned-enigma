About Me

I am a Professor and Chair of the Department of Biostatistics at East Carolina University. With over 30 years of experience in academia, my research focuses on statistical theory, biostatistics, and their applications in various fields including medicine and public health.

Education

  • Ph.D. in Statistics, The University of Chicago, 1987
  • M.S. in Statistics, The University of Chicago, 1986
  • B.A. in Mathematics, Calvin College, 1983

Professional Experience

  • Chair, Department of Biostatistics, East Carolina University (2005-2022)
  • Professor, Department of Biostatistics, East Carolina University (2002-2022)
  • Associate Professor, Department of Biostatistics, East Carolina University (1996-2002)
  • Assistant Professor, Department of Biostatistics, East Carolina University (1994-1996)
  • Assistant Professor, Department of Mathematics, University of Oregon (1987-1994)

Research Interests

  • Statistical Theory
  • Biostatistics
  • Geometric Foundations of Statistical Inference
  • Applications of Statistics in Medical Research

Selected Publications

  • Vos, P. W., & Wu, Q. (2015). Maximum Likelihood Estimators Uniformly Minimize Distribution Variance among Distribution Unbiased Estimators in Exponential Families. Bernoulli, 21, 2120-2138.
  • Vos, P. W., & Anaya-Izquierdo, K. (2014). Using Geometry to Select One Dimensional Exponential Families That Are Monotone Likelihood Ratio in the Sample Space, Are Weakly Unimodal and Can Be Parametrized by a Measure of Central Tendency. Entropy, 16, 4088-4100.
  • Kass, R.E. and Vos, P.W. (1997). Geometrical Foundations of Asymptotic Inference. Wiley.
